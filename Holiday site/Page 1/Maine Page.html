St Patrick’s Day

St Patrick’s Day also known as Feast of Saint Patrick is a religious holiday originating from Ireland, celebrating the day that the Saint Patrick passed away March 17th. St Patrick’s Day is meant to celebrate the arrival of St Patrick and his teachings of Christianity. The holiday is also used to celebrate the culture of the Irish people.

St Patrick’s History

Patrick at the age of sixteen was kidnapped by the Irish and forced into slavery as a shepherd. Six years after being enslaved Patrick returned home to Roman Britain and became a priest. Eventually he returned to Ireland to convert the Pagans into Christianity, he converted thousands of people along the Northern parts of Ireland. After death Patrick became Ireland's foremost Saint.
