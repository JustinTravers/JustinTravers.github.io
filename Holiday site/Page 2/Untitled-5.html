Traditions

Traditionally on St Patrick’s Day people wear the color green as it symbolizes both St Patrick and Ireland as a whole. The symbolism of shamrocks comes from tales of St Patrick explaining the Holy Trinity to the Pagans of Ireland. Around the world people eat and drink mainly traditional irish foods and listen to irish music, and or go to one of many parades.

Stereotypes

Although both wearing green and shamrocks are traditions of St Patrick’s Day they are also viewed as stereotypes because lack of knowledge of the origin of their appearance in the holiday. Realistic stereotypes include every irishman being drunk (although that does happen to many followers of this holiday) but in reality it is a Christian holiday and in tradition you’re not supposed to drink to get drunk and or to have a good time.
